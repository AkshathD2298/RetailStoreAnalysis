Retail Inventory Forecasting & Supply Chain Analytics
Project Overview

Efficient inventory management is critical for retail businesses to balance product availability with operational costs. Poor demand forecasting and inventory allocation often result in stockouts, excess inventory, and lost revenue opportunities.

This project analyzes retail inventory data to evaluate demand forecasting accuracy, stock availability, and inventory efficiency across multiple stores and product categories.

Using data analytics and interactive dashboards, the project identifies operational inefficiencies and provides actionable insights to improve supply chain decision-making.

Business Problem

Retail companies frequently face challenges in maintaining optimal inventory levels due to:

• Inaccurate demand forecasting
• Inefficient inventory distribution across stores
• Overstocking of slow-moving products
• Stockouts for high-demand products

These issues increase operational costs and negatively impact customer experience.

This project explores how data analytics can help identify these inefficiencies and support better inventory planning.

Dataset Information

Dataset: Retail Store Inventory Forecasting Dataset

Records: 73,000+ daily observations

Time period: 2022 – 2024

The dataset contains detailed information about inventory levels, product demand, and store performance.

Key fields include:

Feature	Description
Date	Daily sales record
Store ID	Unique identifier for store
Product ID	Unique identifier for product
Category	Product category
Region	Geographic location
Inventory Level	Available inventory
Units Sold	Daily sales
Units Ordered	Restocking quantity
Demand Forecast	Predicted demand
Price	Product selling price
Analytical Approach

The analysis was conducted using Microsoft Power BI to build an interactive dashboard focusing on three analytical areas.

Key performance indicators were designed to evaluate overall supply chain health:

These metrics provide a quick overview of operational performance.

Inventory Optimization Analysis

This section analyzes how inventory is distributed across stores and product categories.

Demand Forecast Performance

Forecast accuracy was evaluated by comparing predicted demand with actual sales.

The analysis identifies:

• Forecast error trends
• Demand volatility across product categories
• Regions with inconsistent demand patterns

Key Insights
Inventory Imbalance

Some stores maintain significantly higher inventory levels while others frequently experience shortages.

This suggests inefficiencies in inventory distribution and replenishment planning.

Forecast Accuracy Issues

Demand forecasts show notable deviations from actual sales, contributing to both overstock and stockout situations.

Improving forecast models can significantly improve inventory efficiency.

Demand Variability

Certain product categories demonstrate higher demand volatility, requiring more dynamic inventory management strategies.

Business Recommendations

Forecast-Driven Replenishment

Adjust reorder quantities based on predictive demand forecasts.

Category-Based Inventory Strategy

Maintain safety stock for high-volatility product categories.

Regional Inventory Balancing

Align inventory distribution with regional demand patterns.
